I am a researcher working at the intersection of artificial intelligence and neuroscience, with a focus on understanding brain function through computational modeling and, conversely, using insights from neuroscience to inform the development of intelligent systems. My work is inherently interdisciplinary, combining machine learning, cognitive science, and multimodal neuroimaging to study how humans perceive, learn, and create.

I am particularly interested in bridging theoretical and translational research. This includes developing AI-driven methodologies to analyze neural and behavioral data, as well as designing neurotechnology that can be deployed in real-world contexts, especially in health and clinical environments. My research integrates data from EEG, fMRI, and behavioral experiments, aiming to construct models that capture both the structure and dynamics of cognition.

Beyond methodological contributions, I explore domains such as curiosity, aesthetics, and creativity, seeking to understand the mechanisms that drive human exploration and subjective experience. At the same time, I am involved in projects that translate these insights into practical applications, including clinical decision support systems and tools for brain mapping.
